Top 10 Best Malaysian Food

A simple guide to iconic Malaysian dishes you can cook at home. Each card includes a quick recipe summary and tasting notes.

1) Nasi Lemak

Coconut RiceSambalHalal

Recipe (summary)

  • Rice: Rinse, then cook with coconut milk, pandan, ginger & salt.
  • Sambal: Fry blended dried chilies, onion, garlic, belacan; simmer with tamarind & sugar.
  • Serve with fried ikan bilis, peanuts, cucumber & egg.

Comments

Fragrant pandan-coconut rice with a sweet-spicy sambal. The smaller the ikan bilis, the better the crunch.

2) Char Kway Teow

Wok HeiStreet Food

Recipe (summary)

  • Stir-fry garlic, prawns, Chinese sausage; add flat rice noodles.
  • Season with light/dark soy, fish sauce, chili; toss with eggs, chives & sprouts.

Comments

Cook in small batches over high heat to build that smoky wok hei.

3) Asam Laksa

TamarindFish Broth

Recipe (summary)

  • Simmer mackerel with tamarind, lemongrass & torch ginger; blend chilies & shrimp paste into broth.
  • Serve over laksa noodles with cucumber, pineapple, mint & heko.

Comments

Sour, spicy and deeply aromatic — a Penang classic.

4) Roti Canai

FlatbreadBreakfast

Recipe (summary)

  • Knead flour, water, salt, sugar, ghee; rest dough overnight in oil.
  • Stretch paper-thin, coil, flatten and griddle till crisp outside, soft inside.

Comments

Technique-heavy but worth it — serve with dhal, curry or sugar.

5) Satay

GrilledPeanut Sauce

Recipe (summary)

  • Marinate meat with lemongrass, coriander, turmeric, sugar & salt.
  • Grill skewers over charcoal; serve with rich peanut sauce, cucumbers & onions.

Comments

Charcoal smoke + sweet-spiced marinade = unbeatable hawker vibes.

6) Beef Rendang

Slow-CookedCoconut

Recipe (summary)

  • Blend aromatics (shallot, garlic, ginger, galangal, lemongrass, chilies).
  • Simmer beef with spice paste, coconut milk & toasted coconut until dry and dark.

Comments

Layers of spice that intensify as it caramelizes — even better the next day.

7) Hainanese Chicken Rice

Poached ChickenAromatic Rice

Recipe (summary)

  • Poach whole chicken with ginger & spring onion; ice-bath.
  • Fry chicken fat with garlic & ginger; cook rice in stock with pandan.
  • Serve with chilli sauce and ginger-scallion oil.

Comments

Silky chicken, glossy rice, zippy sauces — simple and perfect.

8) Mee Goreng Mamak

MamakStir-fry

Recipe (summary)

  • Toss yellow noodles with garlic, chilies, tofu, potatoes & egg.
  • Season with kicap manis, soy, ketchup & sambal; finish with sprouts and lime.

Comments

Savory-sweet with a spicy kick — classic mamak supper fuel.

9) Nasi Kandar

PenangMixed Curries

Recipe (summary)

  • Steam rice; pair with assorted curries (fish, chicken, beef) and sides.
  • “Banjir” with a mix of gravies for that signature flavor.

Comments

An institution of Penang — the magic is in the curry mix.

10) Cendol

DessertGula Melaka

Recipe (summary)

  • Make pandan jellies (mung bean flour), chill in ice water.
  • Serve with shaved ice, coconut milk & palm sugar syrup; add red beans if you like.

Comments

Cool, creamy, caramel-scented — the perfect Malaysian heat antidote.
