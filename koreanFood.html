Korean Food

Korean cuisine is characterized by its bold flavors, diverse ingredients, and emphasis on balance and harmony. The cuisine is known for its extensive use of fermented foods, particularly kimchi, which is considered Korea's national dish. Key dishes include Bibimbap (mixed rice bowl), Bulgogi (marinated grilled beef), Kimchi (fermented vegetables), and Korean BBQ. Korean food is celebrated for its health benefits, with many dishes featuring vegetables, lean proteins, and fermented ingredients that promote gut health. The cuisine also emphasizes the concept of "bap" (rice) as the center of every meal, accompanied by various side dishes known as "banchan."

Popular Korean Dishes

Bibimbap

Bibimbap is a Korean rice dish. The term "bibim" means "mixing rice" and "bap" means "cooked rice." It's served as a bowl of warm white rice topped with namul (sautéed and seasoned vegetables), gochujang (chili pepper paste), soy sauce, or doenjang (a fermented soybean paste), a raw or fried egg, and sliced meat.

Bulgogi

Bulgogi is a Korean dish that usually consists of grilled marinated beef. The word bulgogi literally means "fire meat" in Korean. The meat is marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and other ingredients before being grilled on a barbecue or on a stove-top griddle.

Learn More

Kimchi

Kimchi is a traditional Korean side dish of salted and fermented vegetables, such as napa cabbage and Korean radish, made with a widely varying selection of seasonings including gochugaru (chili powder), spring onions, garlic, ginger, and jeotgal (salted seafood). It's Korea's national dish.

Learn More

Korean BBQ

Korean BBQ refers to the popular method in Korean cuisine of grilling meat, typically beef, pork, or chicken. The meat is often marinated in a sauce containing soy sauce, garlic, sugar, and sesame oil. The grilled meat is usually wrapped in lettuce or other leafy vegetables along with rice and other side dishes.

Learn More

Japchae

Japchae is a savory and slightly sweet dish of stir-fried glass noodles and vegetables. It is one of Korea's most popular traditional dishes. The noodles are made from sweet potato starch and are mixed with various vegetables and meat, seasoned with soy sauce and sesame oil.

Learn More

Tteokbokki

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean food made from soft rice cake (tteok) and fish cake (eomuk) in a sweet and spicy sauce. It's a common street food in Korea and is often sold by street vendors. The dish is characterized by its bright red-orange color and spicy flavor.
