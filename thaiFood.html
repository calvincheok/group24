Thai Food

Thai cuisine is known for its perfect balance of four fundamental taste senses: sour, sweet, salty, and bitter. The cuisine is characterized by its use of fresh herbs and spices, including lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime, and Thai basil. Key dishes include Pad Thai (stir-fried rice noodles), Tom Yum (spicy and sour soup), Green Curry (spicy coconut milk curry), and Som Tam (spicy green papaya salad). Thai food is celebrated for its vibrant flavors, aromatic ingredients, and the harmonious combination of different taste elements that create a truly unique dining experience.

Popular Thai Dishes

Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a stir-fried rice noodle dish commonly served as a street food and at casual local eateries in Thailand. It is made with rice noodles, eggs, tofu, tamarind pulp, fish sauce, dried shrimp, garlic or shallots, red chili pepper, and palm sugar, served with lime wedges and often chopped roasted peanuts.

Tom Yum

Tom Yum is a hot and sour Thai soup, usually cooked with shrimp. The soup is made from a stock of fresh lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, lime juice, fish sauce, and crushed red chili peppers. It's known for its distinctive hot and sour flavors, with fragrant herbs and generous use of fresh lime juice.

Learn More

Green Curry

Green Curry is a central Thai variety of curry. The name "green" curry derives from the color of the dish, which comes from green chilies. The "sweet" in the name is a reference to the particular color of this curry, not its taste. It's made with coconut milk, green curry paste, meat, and vegetables.

Learn More

Som Tam

Som Tam is a spicy salad made from shredded unripe papaya. It's a popular dish in Thailand and is known for its sweet, sour, and spicy flavors. The dish is made by pounding the ingredients in a mortar and pestle, which helps to release the flavors and create a unique texture.

Learn More

Massaman Curry

Massaman Curry is a rich, relatively mild Thai curry that is influenced by Persian cuisine. It's made with coconut milk, potatoes, onions, and meat (usually beef), and flavored with a unique blend of spices including cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, star anise, cumin, bay leaves, nutmeg, and mace.

Learn More

Mango Sticky Rice

Mango Sticky Rice is a traditional Thai dessert made with glutinous rice, fresh mango, and coconut milk. The rice is steamed and then mixed with sweetened coconut milk, while the mango is served fresh alongside. It's a simple yet delicious dessert that perfectly balances sweet and creamy flavors.
